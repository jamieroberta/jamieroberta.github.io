TRANSMATERIAL INTERCHANGE

Fabricating this project was my first time both 3D printing and laser cutting and I found it very fun and useful! It makes turning your ideas into reality so much easier. I love the way my finished project turned out and I think the plastic working with the wood does in fact make the plastic look malleable!

Final Object, October 31st, 2017

The guidlines for this project were to make a 3D printed "bloblject" that fits uniquely inside of a laser cut cube. I decided I wanted to make the blobject be a malleable objects that in some way forms around the rigid cube. I decided to use a bubble blower because it has a a direct point that it stems from, making the direction of pressure on the cube obvious to understand. I decided to cut three sides of my cube out so the bubble and blower could be seen on all sides.
